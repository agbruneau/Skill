# Commentary Sources Reference

Reference list of commentary series and resources the `sermon-research` skill draws on. The list is organized by **theological priority**, not by publisher.

This skill operates from a **Reformed Baptist + John MacArthur** lens. The sources below are tiered to reflect that: Tier 1 is consulted first and weighted most heavily, Tier 2 fills out exegetical breadth, Tier 3 is used with discernment.

---

## Tier 1 — Primary Lens (MacArthur + Reformed)

These are the sources whose readings shape the default interpretation.

### MacArthur (the primary expository voice)

| Name | Description | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **MacArthur New Testament Commentary** (Moody, 33+ vols.) | John MacArthur's verse-by-verse exposition of the entire NT. The default Tier 1 reference for any NT passage. Direct, application-driven, dispensational, Lordship-salvation framing. | Paid. Print, Logos, partial free excerpts via [gty.org](https://www.gty.org). |
| **The MacArthur Study Bible** (NASB / ESV / LSB) | One-volume study notes by MacArthur. Faster lookup than the full commentary; same theological frame. The **LSB** edition (Legacy Standard Bible) is the translation MacArthur and Master's Seminary now use by default. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **The MacArthur Bible Commentary** | Single-volume condensation covering the whole Bible (OT and NT). Useful when there is no full MacArthur NT volume on the passage, or for OT work. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Grace To You sermon archive** | Free transcripts and audio of MacArthur's sermons going back to 1969, covering most of the NT verse by verse. Often the cleanest record of MacArthur's actual exposition of a given passage. | Free. [gty.org](https://www.gty.org). |

### Calvin and the magisterial Reformers

| Name | Description | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Calvin's Commentaries** | John Calvin (1509-1564) on most of the Bible. Foundational text of the Reformed tradition, still cited five centuries later. Exceptional theological depth and pastoral clarity. | Public domain. Free via [CCEL](https://ccel.org/ccel/calvin), Blue Letter Bible, Bible Hub. |
| **Calvin's Institutes of the Christian Religion** | Not a commentary per se, but the indispensable systematic companion when the passage touches doctrine. Cite by book/chapter/section. | Public domain. CCEL. |
| **Martin Luther — selected commentaries** (esp. Galatians, Romans) | Useful for justification-heavy passages and the Pauline corpus, with care: Luther is not Reformed in the later confessional sense. | Public domain (older translations). |

### The Puritan and 17th-century Reformed Baptist witness

| Name | Description | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **John Owen — Works** (16 vols., Banner of Truth) | The summit of Puritan theological writing. Especially: *The Death of Death in the Death of Christ* (particular redemption), *Communion with God*, *Mortification of Sin*, the *Hebrews* commentary (7 vols.), *Pneumatologia* (the Holy Spirit). Use Owen anywhere the passage touches sin, sanctification, the atonement, or pneumatology. | Print (Banner of Truth). [CCEL](https://ccel.org/ccel/owen) for older editions. |
| **John Bunyan — Works** | *Pilgrim's Progress* and *The Holy War* are doctrinally rich allegories; the lesser-known treatises (*The Pharisee and the Publican*, *Justification by an Imputed Righteousness*) are sermon gold. Particular Baptist. | Public domain. CCEL. |
| **Matthew Henry's Commentary on the Whole Bible** | Matthew Henry (1662-1714), English Presbyterian. Warm, devotional, with strong practical application. Classic source for quotations and illustrations. | Public domain. Free online; print in abridged and complete editions. |
| **John Gill — Exposition of the Old and New Testaments** | John Gill (1697-1771), Particular Baptist pastor (Spurgeon's predecessor at New Park Street). Verse-by-verse on the entire Bible, deeply Reformed Baptist, strong on Hebrew and rabbinics. | Public domain. Free via Bible Study Tools, Sacred-Texts. |
| **Jonathan Edwards — Works** (Banner of Truth, 2 vols.; Yale ed., 26 vols.) | Edwards is not a commentator per se, but his sermons (*Sinners in the Hands of an Angry God*, *A Divine and Supernatural Light*, *The Justice of God in the Damnation of Sinners*) and treatises (*Religious Affections*, *Freedom of the Will*) shape Reformed preaching on the affections, the will, and divine sovereignty. | Print and free at [edwards.yale.edu](https://edwards.yale.edu). |
| **Thomas Watson, Thomas Brooks, Thomas Goodwin, Stephen Charnock** | Banner of Truth Puritan paperbacks. Charnock's *The Existence and Attributes of God* is the gold standard on theology proper. | Print (Banner of Truth). |

### Spurgeon (the prince of Reformed Baptist preachers)

| Name | Description | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Charles Spurgeon — sermons** (63 volumes, *Metropolitan Tabernacle Pulpit* and *New Park Street Pulpit*) | Roughly 3,500 sermons from London's largest 19th-century pulpit. The single richest preaching corpus in the Reformed Baptist tradition. Search the archive for the passage; you will rarely come up empty. | Public domain. Free at [spurgeon.org](https://www.spurgeon.org/resource-library/sermons), CCEL, [Spurgeon's Sermons](https://www.spurgeongems.org). |
| **The Treasury of David** | Spurgeon's verse-by-verse exposition of the Psalms, drawing on Puritan and Reformed sources. The default Tier 1 reference for any Psalms passage. | Public domain. Free at CCEL, [Christian Classics](https://www.ccel.org/ccel/spurgeon/treasury). |
| **Lectures to My Students** | Not a commentary but the classic on Reformed Baptist preaching. Worth re-reading annually. | Public domain. |

### Contemporary Reformed and Reformed Baptist exegesis

| Name | Description | Access |
|---|---|---|
| **Thomas Schreiner — commentaries** | Reformed Baptist NT scholar (Southern Seminary). *Romans* (BECNT), *Galatians* (ZECNT), *1 Corinthians* (ZECNT), *Hebrews* (BTCP), *1-2 Peter, Jude* (NAC), *The King in His Beauty* (whole-Bible biblical theology). The contemporary first-stop after MacArthur for serious exegesis. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Reformed Expository Commentary (REC)** (P&R) | Preaching-oriented Reformed commentaries by Philip Ryken, Richard Phillips, Iain Duguid, Daniel Doriani, Derek Thomas, Daniel Akin, and others. Designed specifically for Christ-centered expository preaching. Excellent template for how a Reformed preacher works through a text. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Geneva Series of Commentaries** (Banner of Truth) | Reprints of Reformed and Puritan classics: Calvin on Romans and the minor prophets, Charles Hodge on the Pauline epistles, John Brown on Hebrews, Robert Candlish on 1 John, etc. Standard furniture in a Reformed pastoral library. | Paid. Print. |
| **Welwyn Commentary Series** (Evangelical Press) | Accessible Reformed exposition; useful when REC has not yet covered the book. Authors include Stuart Olyott, Geoffrey Thomas. | Paid. Print. |
| **Kistemaker / Hendriksen — New Testament Commentary** (Baker) | Older Reformed commentary set, complete on the NT. Solid, occasionally dated, theologically reliable. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **MacArthur Bible Studies / Master's Seminary monographs** (Sam Waldron, Nathan Busenitz, Mike Riccardi, Iain Murray's biographies) | Tighter exposition and pastoral theology from MacArthur's circle. | Paid. Print. |

### Reformed systematics (companion volumes for doctrinal weight)

These are not commentaries but should be cited alongside the exegesis when a passage carries clear dogmatic weight.

| Name | Description |
|---|---|
| **Joel Beeke and Paul Smalley — *Reformed Systematic Theology*** (4 vols., Crossway) | The most thorough contemporary Reformed systematic. Confessionally located, pastorally written. |
| **Wayne Grudem — *Systematic Theology*** (2nd ed., Zondervan) | Reformed Baptist, accessible, widely used in the MacArthur circle. Continuationist on gifts (the major caveat). |
| **Louis Berkhof — *Systematic Theology*** | Classic 20th-century Reformed systematic. |
| **Robert Reymond — *A New Systematic Theology of the Christian Faith*** | Confessional Presbyterian; rigorous on the doctrine of God and Christology. |
| **John Frame — *Systematic Theology*** | Reformed; helpful on theological method and ethics. |
| **Herman Bavinck — *Reformed Dogmatics*** (4 vols.) | The deepest Reformed dogmatics of the 20th century. Slow but rewarding. |
| **R.C. Sproul — *Reformation Study Bible* notes** | One-volume Reformed study notes. Sproul's voice on the doctrines of grace is invaluable. |

### Confessional documents (always within reach)

| Document | Use |
|---|---|
| **1689 London Baptist Confession of Faith** | The historic Reformed Baptist confession. Cite chapter and paragraph when a passage maps to a confessional locus. Note where the **1689 covenantal** position diverges from MacArthur's dispensationalism (esp. ch. 7 on covenants, ch. 32 on last things). |
| **Westminster Confession of Faith / Larger Catechism / Shorter Catechism** | The Presbyterian parallel. Where the 1689 borrows directly from Westminster, citing both is fair game. |
| **Heidelberg Catechism, Belgic Confession, Canons of Dort** | Continental Reformed standards. The Canons of Dort are the definitive confessional treatment of the doctrines of grace. |

## Tier 2 — Supplementary Exegesis (Use to Sharpen, Not to Set the Frame)

These commentaries are exegetically valuable and largely Reformed-friendly. They fill out word studies, grammatical analysis, and historical background. They do not override Tier 1 on doctrinal questions.

| Name | Publisher | Description | Access |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Pillar New Testament Commentary** (PNTC) | Eerdmans | Edited by D.A. Carson. Carson, Andreas Köstenberger, Peter O'Brien, Douglas Moo. Largely Reformed sensibility, written for ministers. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **NICNT** (New International Commentary on the New Testament) | Eerdmans | Long-running evangelical academic series. Quality varies by volume. Strong volumes: Moo on Romans, Bruce on Hebrews, Fee on 1 Corinthians (continuationist — flag and engage critically). | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **NICOT** (New International Commentary on the Old Testament) | Eerdmans | OT counterpart. Strong volumes: Stuart on Hosea-Jonah, Block on Ezekiel and Judges-Ruth. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **BECNT** (Baker Exegetical) | Baker Academic | Detailed grammatical analysis of the Greek. Schreiner (Romans), Köstenberger (John), Bock (Luke, Acts). | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **ZECNT** (Zondervan Exegetical Commentary on the NT) | Zondervan | Includes a "theology in application" section per pericope. Schreiner (Galatians, 1 Corinthians), Köstenberger. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **WBC** (Word Biblical Commentary) | Zondervan / HarperCollins | Verse-by-verse academic series. Quality varies; some volumes are critically liberal. Use with discernment. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **NSBT** (New Studies in Biblical Theology) | IVP / Apollos | Edited by D.A. Carson. Topical biblical-theological monographs (Schreiner on the Law, Beale on the Temple, Williamson on covenant). Excellent for whole-Bible synthesis. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Mentor Commentary Series** | Christian Focus | Scottish Reformed exegetical series. John Currid, Iain Duguid, Dale Ralph Davis. Solid on original languages. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Tyndale Old / New Testament Commentaries** | IVP | Brief, accessible, evangelical. Useful for quick orientation. | Paid. Print. |
| **NAC** (New American Commentary) | B&H | Southern Baptist 40+ volume series. Quality varies; the volumes by Schreiner (1-2 Peter, Jude), Köstenberger, Akin are Tier 1 in Reformed Baptist circles. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Holman New Testament Commentary** | B&H | Pastoral series for pastors without advanced Greek. Useful for second opinions. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Christ-Centered Exposition (CCE)** | B&H Academic | Designed to surface the gospel thread through every passage. Use selectively — strong on redemptive-historical reading, occasionally light on doctrinal precision. | Paid. Print and Logos. |
| **Preaching the Word** | Crossway | Edited by R. Kent Hughes. Expository sermons in print. Excellent for seeing how a skilled preacher worked through a text. Largely Reformed sensibility. | Paid. Print and Crossway website. |

## Tier 3 — Read with Discernment

These sources are useful but carry theological commitments that diverge from the Tier 1 frame. Use them for what they do well; do not let them set the doctrinal lens.

| Name | What it does well | What to flag |
|---|---|---|
| **NIV Application Commentary (NIVAC)** | Three-part structure (original meaning, bridging contexts, contemporary significance) is a solid template for application. | Application sections often drift toward broad evangelical framing rather than Reformed specificity. Use the *original meaning* sections; reframe application yourself. |
| **BE Series (Warren Wiersbe)** | Plain-English, illustration-rich, devotional. | Light on technical exegesis. Not Reformed. Useful for second-tier color, not for primary interpretation. |
| **Tom Wright / N.T. Wright — *For Everyone* series, *The New Testament for Everyone*** | Vivid prose, sharp historical sense. | Wright is the leading voice of the **New Perspective on Paul**, which redefines justification in a covenantal/communal direction that conflicts with the Reformation reading. Engage critically; do not adopt his frame on Romans, Galatians, or Philippians. |
| **Interpretation: A Bible Commentary for Teaching and Preaching** | Mainline Presbyterian (PCUSA), strong on biblical theology in a few volumes. | Often critically liberal on authorship, historicity, and inerrancy. Read in dialogue with Tier 1, not as an authority. |
| **Hermeneia, Anchor Bible, ICC** | Maximally rigorous on text-critical and historical questions. | Frequently rationalist, sometimes hostile to evangelical commitments. Mine for technical detail; do not import their interpretive frame. |
| **Continuationist authors on 1 Cor 12-14, Acts 2, Mark 16** (Fee, Carson on the gifts) | Detailed grammatical analysis. | Their conclusions on the cessation of sign-gifts diverge from the cessationist position this skill defaults to (per MacArthur, *Strange Fire*). State the cessationist reading, name the alternative, do not adopt it. |

## How This Skill Uses These Sources

The skill defaults to **Tier 1 for the interpretive frame** and pulls **Tier 2 to fill out exegetical detail** (grammar, lexicon, background, comparison points). Tier 3 is consulted to be informed of alternative readings, never to set the lens.

When citing a position by name, the skill names the commentator. It does not fabricate direct quotes; if only the position is documented, it summarizes rather than invents words. When MacArthur and confessional Reformed Baptist (1689 LBCF) readings diverge — most often on eschatology, Israel/Church, the millennium — both are named and the divergence is acknowledged rather than papered over.

If you have direct access through Logos, gty.org, spurgeon.org, CCEL, or print, reading the original source after the research summary is always worth the time.
